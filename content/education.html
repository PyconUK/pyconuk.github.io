Education is at the heart of the Python community, after all, Guido van Rossum himself (the inventor of Python) tells us that Python was created as a language for anyone to use.

For the fourth year in a row, we're organising a set of activities for learners of all ages, abilities and backgrounds. The Python community is open to all and we want to support, cherish and foster our newest members.

We have something for everyone:

  • Teacher training
  • Kids coding
  • Trans*Code
  • Django Girls

On Friday

Teachers

We look forward to welcoming our teaching colleagues for a day of training, collaboration, resource writing and networking in a friendly yet fun environment where we aim to help teachers get the most out of Python.

The picture above is from a workshop for both teachers and developers where we all learned about object oriented programming.

This year we're welcoming back the Raspberry Pi Foundation education team and Jam Packed to help us run the training.

Sign up here and purchase a teacher's ticket.

Trans*Code

Trans*Code is for trans* people and allies who want to draw attention to issues facing women and the transgender community while providing introductory programming workshops. Participants drawn from all skill levels and backgrounds are welcome.

The video above (created for ComputerWeekly) gives you a flavour of the sorts of things you can expect from the day.

To register your interest in attending please fill in this form.

Django Girls

Django Girls will be running a Python and Django workshop designed to inspire women to fall in love with programming.

Their mission is to bring more amazing women into the world of technology and increase the diversity of our community. They believe that technology is for people and everyone should be able to build it. They make technology more approachable by creating simple tools and resources designed with empathy.

To register your interest in attending please fill in this form.

Saturday

Kids' Day

We will be holding a Raspberry Jam where participants of all ages will get to play, code, learn and share all things programming in Python on a RaspberryPi and any other device we can get to work.


We aim to run various workshops aimed at young beginner Pythonistas throughout the day. These will include web design, robotics, Minecraft and physical computing. Our aim is to turn excited kids into exhausted but inspired proto-programmers. :-)
